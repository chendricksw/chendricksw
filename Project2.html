Define Problem Statement and Objectives¶

E-News Express aims to expand business by acquiring new subscribers. The company plans to study user interest to better drive engagement. The design team has developed a new landing page. To test the effectiveness an experiment was conducted by randomly selecting 100 users and dividing them equally into two groups. Group one (control) was served the existing page while group two (treatment) was served the new page. Data from these groups have been collected to determine the effectiveness of the new page at a significance level of 5%. The following questions are to be answered:

Do the users spend more time on the new landing page than on the existing landing page?

Is the conversion rate (the proportion of users who visit the landing page and get converted) for the new page greater than the conversion rate for the old page?

Does the converted status depend on the preferred language?

Is the time spent on the new page the same for the different language users?

Data Dictionary:

user_id - Unique user ID of the person visiting the website

group - Whether the user belongs to the first group (control) or the second group (treatment)

landing_page - Whether the landing page is new or old

time_spent_on_the_page - Time (in minutes) spent by the user on the landing page

converted - Whether the user gets converted to a subscriber of the news portal or not

language_preferred - Language chosen by the user to view the landing page

Step 1: Define the null and alternate hypotheses¶

Let $\mu$ be the mean time spent on the landing page. With $\mu$1 being the Avg for the existing page and $\mu$2 being the Avg for the new page.

$H_0$: $\mu$1 = $\mu$2

$H_a$: $\mu$1 < $\mu$2

Step 2: Select Appropriate test¶

Two independent sample t-test

Assumptions:

Continuous Data - Yes, time spent is continuous.

Normally distributed population and Sample size < 30 - Yes, the population is normal and the sample size is 50.

Observations are from a simple random sample - Yes, we are informed that the collection is a random sample.

Population standard deviation is unknown

Step 3: Decide the significance level¶

We are instructed to use a significance level of 5%.

Step 6: Compare the p-value with $\alpha$¶

A p-value (~0.0001) is very small and is less than the level of significance of 0.05. Meaning that we can reject the null hypothesis.

Step 7: Draw inference¶

As the p-value(~0.0001) is much smaller than the level of significance, we can reject the null hypothesis. We do have enough significance to conclude that there is a difference between time spent on the new page versus the old page.

Define null and alternative hypothesis

Let $\mu_1, \mu_2, \mu_3$ be the means of time spent on the page for different language users (Spanish, English, French).

$H_0: \mu_1 = \mu_2 = \mu_3$

$H_a: $ At least one level is different from the rest.

One way ANOVA F-test

Assumptions:

The populations are normally distributed - We will test with Shapiro-Wilk's Test

Sample are independent simple random sample - Yes, we are informed samples collected are simple random samples.

Populations variances are equal - We will test with Levene's Test

Shapiro-Wilk's test for normality

H$_0$ : Time spent on the page follows a normal distribution

H$_a$ : Time spent on the page does not follow a normal distribution

In [17]:
#Shapiro-Wilk's test for normality
from scipy import stats

w, p_value = stats.shapiro(df['time_spent_on_the_page'])
print('The p-value is', p_value)

The p-value is 0.5643684267997742

The p-value (~0.56) is large, hence we fail to reject the null hypothesis. Meaning time spent on the page follows a normal distribution.

Levene's Test for equality of variances

H$_0$ : All the population variances are equal

H$_a$ : At least one population variance is not equal

In [18]:
#Levene's Test
from scipy.stats import levene

statistic, p_value = levene(df['time_spent_on_the_page'][df['language_preferred']== 'Spanish'],
                            df['time_spent_on_the_page'][df['language_preferred']== 'English'],
                            df['time_spent_on_the_page'][df['language_preferred']== 'French'])

print('The p-value is', p_value)

The p-value is 0.06515086840327314

The p-value (~0.07) is greater than the level of significance (0.05), therefore we fail to reject the null hypothesis. Meaning that we can conclude that the population variances are equal.

Now knowing the assumptions are supported, we can carry on with the one-way ANOVA F-test. Let's revisit the hypotheses below:

Let $\mu_1, \mu_2, \mu_3$ be the means of time spent on the page for different language users (Spanish, English, French). With a level of significance of 5%.

$H_0: \mu_1 = \mu_2 = \mu_3$

$H_a: $ At least one level is different from the rest.
